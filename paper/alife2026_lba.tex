\begin{abstract}
Cellular automata (CAs) often produce extended periodic domains separated by
localized defects, particles, and domain boundaries. We present
\emph{Winnower}, a tool that infers a periodic domain from a finite CA
space--time diagram. Each candidate temporal period and lattice displacement
partitions the observed sites into translation orbits; majority vote on each
orbit gives the Hamming-nearest domain template, and candidates are ranked by
Bernoulli normalized maximum likelihood (NML). The output is a compact domain
template plus a candidate-defect mask. In preliminary 1D/2D/3D experiments the
selected symmetry settles after a short transient, and the NML complexity term
prevents the period inflation seen with fit-only selection: across 105
nontrivial Life-like rules, NML selected period~1 for 97, while unpenalized
likelihood always chose the largest scanned period. Winnower is a lightweight
first pass for CA surveys, proposing candidate domains before local particle
or coherent-structure analysis. Data and code:
\url{https://github.com/zitterbewegung/Winnower}.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
A cellular automaton (CA) evolves a lattice by applying the same local rule at
every cell. Many rules produce \emph{spatiotemporal domains} --- regions
invariant under a combined temporal translation and lattice displacement ---
threaded with particles, gliders, and domain boundaries. The domain is the
reference frame that makes such structure legible, so inferring it from a
finite observation is a natural first step for artificial-life analyses.

Local causal-state methods reconstruct spatiotemporal organization in detail
\citep{rupe2024}, while CA taxonomies, transient analyses, and
compression-based approaches characterize whole rules
\citep{vispoel2022,rollier2024,hudcova2021,alfaro2024,cisneros2023}. Winnower
occupies a middle ground: a global, complexity-penalized selector of periodic
domain templates, fast enough for broad rule-space sweeps.

\section{Method}
Given a binary space--time block $U$, a period $p$, and a displacement
$\bs$, Winnower fits templates satisfying
$B[t{+}p,\,\mathbf{x}{+}\bs \bmod \bD]=B[t,\mathbf{x}]$. The translation
partitions the observed sites into translation orbits $\{O_j\}$; assigning
each orbit its majority state yields the template with minimum Hamming
disagreement, and the mismatched sites form the defect mask
(\cref{fig:winnower-pipeline}). Candidates are ranked by the Bernoulli NML
score
%
\begin{equation}
\NML(p,\bs)=\sum_j n_j\,\Hb(\hat\theta_j)\;+\;\tfrac12\sum_j \log_2 n_j,
\label{eq:nml}
\end{equation}
%
with $n_j=|O_j|$, $\hat\theta_j=n_j^{(1)}/n_j$ the in-state-1 fraction, and
$\Hb$ binary entropy: an NLL fit term plus the asymptotic NML complexity of
the orbit models \citep{shtarkov1987,grunwald2007,yamanishi2023}.

The penalty is essential: a velocity-matched longer period refines the orbit
partition, so it can only improve raw fit, and fit-only selection inflates the
period even when the extra orbits encode nothing new. We machine-checked this
divisibility/orbit-refinement relation in Lean~4 under public CI
\citep{achim2025}. Once candidates differ in per-site NLL rate, the linear fit
gap dominates the logarithmic penalty gap, so the selection settles and the
shortest equivalent candidate is recovered when it is in the search set.

\section{Preliminary Results}
We tested ECAs 30, 54, and 110; 2D Life-like and totalistic rules; and 3D
Life B5/S45 \citep{bays1987,bays2006}. \Cref{fig:results} shows the
decompositions and horizon sweep ($T$ up to $800$ at seed~11 in 1D/2D; up to
$50$ at seed~42 in 3D, where the soup decays toward the quiescent domain).
Selection settled immediately for ECA-54 (period~4, margin 681 to 2318 bits)
and 3D Life (period~1, 3324 to 7403 bits), and after one transition for
ECA-110 ($1\!\to\!7$, the Ether domain) and S24/B11 ($1\!\to\!2$); margins
grew after the last change. At $T{=}100$ on 105 nontrivial Life-like rules,
unpenalized NLL selected the largest scanned period every time, while NML
chose period~1 for 97 --- the orbit-refinement effect above. The defect mask
is not noise: entropy-rate comparisons show the template captures the
predictable component while defects retain structured variation, and in rules
such as S37/B11 the defect population persists and scales with lattice size
\citep{secktuohmora2023}.

\paragraph{Reproducibility.} Every number and figure regenerates from the
released tool: \texttt{analyze}/\texttt{analyze2d}/\texttt{analyze3d} for
single rules; \cref{fig:results} via \texttt{alife\_lba\_figures.py} after
\texttt{convergence\_all\_dims.py}.

\section{Discussion and Future Work}
Winnower reduces each run to a selected translation symmetry, a periodic
template, and a defect mask. It assumes binary states, searches a supplied
candidate set, and complements rather than replaces local causal-state
reconstruction \citep{rupe2024,rupe2025}. Future work: robustness to
observation length, noise, and candidate-set size; connecting defect masks to
local tracking; and extending to multistate, continuous, and neural CAs
\citep{hamon2024,plantec2025,niklasson2021}.
