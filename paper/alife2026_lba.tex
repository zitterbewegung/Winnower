\begin{abstract}
Cellular automata (CAs) often generate space--time diagrams comprising broad
spatiotemporal domains together with defects, particles, and domain boundaries.
We introduce \emph{Winnower}, a software tool and model-selection framework for
inferring such domains from finite observations. Each candidate pair of
temporal period and lattice displacement defines a relative-periodic
translation symmetry and partitions observed space--time sites into
translation orbits. Winnower assigns each orbit its majority cell state,
yielding the Hamming-nearest binary domain template, and ranks candidates
by the Bernoulli normalized maximum likelihood (NML) criterion. It returns
an inferred periodic domain template and a domain residual represented as a
candidate-defect mask. Across one-, two-, and three-dimensional CAs, the
selected symmetry stabilizes after short
transients, the complexity term suppresses spurious period inflation, and the
domain residual highlights rules with persistent localized structure. Winnower
is a global preprocessing method for artificial-life studies of CA dynamics:
it proposes a compact domain hypothesis for large rule-space sweeps and leaves
candidate particles and coherent structures for subsequent local analysis.
Data and code: \url{https://github.com/zitterbewegung/Winnower}.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
A cellular automaton (CA) evolves a lattice configuration by synchronously
applying a local update rule at every site; iteration produces a space--time
diagram. Many CAs exhibit extended \emph{spatiotemporal domains}---regions
invariant under a combined temporal translation and lattice displacement---
alongside defects, particles or gliders, and domain boundaries. In artificial
life, domains provide the regular organization against which information
transport, coherent structures, and emergent computation become visible. A
practical question follows: given a finite CA space--time diagram, which
periodic translation symmetry gives the simplest adequate domain model, and
which cell states remain unexplained by it?

Computational mechanics and local causal-state methods reconstruct local
spatiotemporal organization in detail \citep{rupe2024}, while CA taxonomies,
transient analyses, and compression-based methods characterize whole rules and
global pattern complexity
\citep{vispoel2022,rollier2024,hudcova2021,alfaro2024,cisneros2023}. Winnower
occupies a middle ground: global, complexity-penalized periodic domain inference
fast enough for broad CA rule-space sweeps.

\section{The Winnower Algorithm}
Given a finite binary CA space--time block $U$, a temporal period $p$, and a
lattice-displacement vector $\bs$, Winnower fits domain templates satisfying
$B[t{+}p,\,\mathbf{x}{+}\bs \bmod \bD]=B[t,\mathbf{x}]$, where $\bD$ gives the
lattice dimensions. The domain therefore repeats after $p$ synchronous updates
up to displacement $\bs$. This translation partitions observed space--time
sites into translation orbits $\{O_j\}$. Assigning each orbit its majority cell
state produces the Hamming-nearest binary template in one linear pass per
candidate symmetry. Candidates are ranked by the Bernoulli NML score
%
\begin{equation}
\NML(p,\bs)=\sum_j n_j\,\Hb(\hat\theta_j)\;+\;\tfrac12\sum_j \log_2 n_j,
\label{eq:nml}
\end{equation}
%
where $n_j=|O_j|$, $\hat\theta_j=n_j^{(1)}/n_j$, and $\Hb$ is binary entropy: a
Bernoulli negative log-likelihood (NLL) fit term plus the asymptotic NML
complexity of the independent translation-orbit models
\citep{shtarkov1987,grunwald2007,yamanishi2023}.

The complete inference pipeline is summarized in
\cref{fig:winnower-pipeline}.

The penalty is essential. A velocity-matched longer-period candidate refines
the translation-orbit partition of its shorter-period counterpart, so it can
only reduce the Hamming residual and NLL. Fit-only selection therefore inflates
the period along every such refinement chain, even when the extra orbits encode
no new domain structure; the second term of \cref{eq:nml} counteracts this
pressure. Velocity-matched divisibility is precisely the condition for
universal refinement, a structural result machine-checked in Lean~4 under
public CI \citep{achim2025}. Once candidates have distinct per-site NLL
rates, the linear fit gap dominates the logarithmic penalty gap.
The selected symmetry then stabilizes and, when the generating domain is in the
candidate set, recovers its smallest equivalent period after a finite transient.


\section{Experiments}
Winnower was evaluated on one-dimensional elementary cellular automata (ECAs;
rules 30, 54, and 110), two-dimensional Life-like and totalistic CA surveys,
and a three-dimensional totalistic CA \citep{bays1987,bays2006}. Selected
periods stabilize immediately (ECA rule~54 at period~4 and the three-dimensional
rule at period~1) or after short transients (ECA rule~110 and S24/B11). Every
tested CA changes its selected candidate at most twice, and the winning margin
grows after the final transition. Of Hamming residual, Bernoulli NLL, and
Bernoulli NML, only NML suppresses period inflation; NLL
selects the largest scanned period for all 105 rules.

The domain residual is not merely noise. Entropy-rate comparisons indicate that
the inferred domain captures the predictable component of the space--time
diagram while the residual retains structured uncertainty. Two-dimensional
rules such as S37/B11 produce persistent residual populations that scale with
lattice size, consistent with localized structures targeted by particle,
glider, and coherent-structure detectors \citep{secktuohmora2023}.

\section{Discussion and Conclusion}
Winnower casts periodic domain inference as penalized model selection over CA
spatiotemporal translation symmetries. Translation orbits make fitting fast,
while NML explains unpenalized overfitting and eventual selection stability. It
does not replace local causal-state reconstruction \citep{rupe2024,rupe2025} or
claim that every inferred symmetry is a unique intrinsic domain. It returns a
compact candidate domain, a candidate-defect mask, and a principled comparison
across CA rule families. The same role extends to continuous cellular automata
and neural cellular automata \citep{hamon2024,plantec2025,niklasson2021}.
Winnower is therefore a scalable preprocessing stage for artificial-life
analyses of emergent organization in CA space--time diagrams, not a complete
account of local dynamics or particle semantics.
