# Editor brief for a mathematics PhD


## Venue target
This is being prepared for the **ALIFE 2026 Late-Breaking Abstract** track.

That means the manuscript should read like:
- a strong 2-page poster abstract
- visually guided and intuitive
- phenomenon-first, not theorem-first
- interesting enough to make readers want to visit the poster

## What to improve
Please focus on:
1. making the opening feel less robotic
2. shortening long sentences
3. improving paragraph transitions
4. reducing repeated phrasing such as “the method is,” “the framework is,” or “the contribution is”
5. making the “Why this matters for ALIFE” paragraph sound more natural
6. making the findings paragraph easier to scan quickly

## What to preserve
Please preserve:
- the mathematical meaning
- the equations
- the examples and empirical claims
- the figure captions unless wording can be improved without changing content
- the distinction between what is classical and what is new

## Audience
Assume the reader is:
- comfortable with artificial life and cellular automata
- not necessarily committed to MDL/NML theory
- looking for a clear idea, not a proof-heavy paper

## Tone target
Please make it sound like:
- a clear scientific abstract written by a human
- concise and confident
- not hype-heavy
- not overly formal or legalistic
- not LLM-generic

## Editing rule
If a sentence sounds mathematically correct but awkward, please rewrite it.
If a sentence raises a substantive technical issue, please leave a margin comment instead of silently changing the mathematics.
